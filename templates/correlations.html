How each component affects one another?

Beta is the slope of the line that best fits the data against the index. It is a historical measure of the stock's volatilities against the factor. For S&P500 is the a good measure of the market as a whole. When looking at S&P500, the return generated from a portfolio can be attributed to overall market returns

  • B = 1 move with market
  • B > 1 more volatile than the market (high tech)
  • B < 1 less volatile than the market (utility companies)
  • B < 0 inverse of the market (gold)
  • B = 0 cash, doesn't change with the market

There are two caveats of in using beta

  • Beta is a measurement of systematic risk, which is the risk that the market as a whole faces. It is based on the effect of market wide risk, not the risk of the company
  • Beta is a historical measure of stock's volatility, this means that past volatility doesn't necessary predict future beta or future volatility. Study of Gene Fama and Ken French: Cross-section of expected stock returns, exam reliability of past beta concluded that for individual stocks paste beta is not a good predictor of future beta. Beta tends to revert back to 1.
